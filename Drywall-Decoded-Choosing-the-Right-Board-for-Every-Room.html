Drywall Decoded

Choosing the Right Board for Every Room

When you look at a finished room, all you see is smooth, painted walls. But what’s behind that paint matters more than you think. Using the wrong type of drywall in a damp bathroom or a high-heat kitchen can lead to mold, cracks, or safety hazards.

At Cornerstone Constructs, we make it easy. Tell us which room you’re finishing, and we’ll match it with the perfect "armor" for your walls.

Why the Right Drywall Matters

  • Health & Safety: Specialized boards prevent mold growth and provide extra fire protection.
  • Durability: High-traffic areas need boards that resist dents and impacts.
  • Sound Proofing: The right thickness or layering can make noisy rooms much quieter.

Step 1: Pick Your Room

Living Rooms, Bedrooms & Wardrobes

Our Choice: Standard Grey / White Board

  • Cost-effective and easy to install
  • Provides a perfectly flat surface for painting
  • Ideal for gallery walls and main living spaces

Bathrooms & Kitchens

Our Choice: Moisture Resistant (Greenboard)

  • Treated with water-resistant coating
  • Prevents mold in steamy environments
  • Perfect for showers, tubs, and kitchen walls

Utility Rooms & Garages

Our Choice: Fire Rated (Type X)

  • Thicker, denser board with glass fibers
  • Provides significantly longer fire resistance
  • Essential near furnaces, water heaters, and electrical panels

Basements & Wet Zones

Our Choice: Cement Board (Concrete Grey)

  • Contains no organic material — mold resistant
  • Virtually indestructible in high-moisture areas
  • Excellent base for tile in showers and basements

Step 2: Understand the Materials

  • Standard Grey/White: The versatile all-star for 80% of your home.
  • Moisture Resistant Green: The shield against humidity and steam.
  • Fire Rated (Type X): Extra thick and extra safe.
  • Cement Board: The heavyweight for tile backing and wet areas.

💡 Pro-Tips for a Flawless Finish

  • Smoothness Secret: Even the best drywall looks bad without perfect taping and mudding. We specialize in Level 5 finishes — invisible seams under bright lights.
  • Sound Control: Ask about sound-dampening techniques for home theaters or bedrooms near noisy areas.
  • Small Repairs: Don’t replace an entire wall for a doorknob hole or water damage. Our seamless patch repairs blend perfectly.
