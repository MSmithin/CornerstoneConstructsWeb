Soffit & Fascia: The Unsung Heroes of Your Roof

They may be hidden, but they protect everything underneath

If your roof is a hat, the soffit and fascia are the trim that keeps it secure and looking sharp. While they might seem like minor details, they play a massive role in keeping your attic cool and your rafters dry.

Here is a quick guide to the two main ways we protect the "edges" of your home.

First off: What’s the difference?

  • Soffit: The material tucked underneath the roof overhang. If you stood against your house and looked straight up, you’d be looking at the soffit. Its main job is to “breathe” (provide ventilation) for your attic.
  • Fascia: The vertical board that faces out. It’s what your gutters are attached to. Its main job is to act as a barrier against rain and pests.

Our Material Options

1. Aluminum Soffit — The "Heavy Duty" Choice

Very clean, sharp lines. Looks great on modern or high-end homes.

  • Pros: Incredibly durable, won’t sag, non-combustible, and offers excellent airflow for attic cooling.
  • Maintenance: Practically zero — just an occasional hose down.
  • Best For: Homeowners who want maximum protection and a premium finish.

2. Vinyl Soffit — The "Flexible" Choice

Mimics the look of painted wood without the maintenance.

  • Pros: More affordable, dent-resistant, excellent moisture and salt-air resistance.
  • Maintenance: Very low — never needs painting.
  • Best For: Cost-effective, great-looking solution with solid performance.

Why We Always Use Aluminum Fascia

No matter which soffit you choose, we pair it with Aluminum Fascia because it takes the brunt of the weather. Benefits include:

  • No more painting — ever
  • Rock-solid gutter support
  • Critter-proof protection against squirrels and birds
  • Will not rot, warp, or peel
