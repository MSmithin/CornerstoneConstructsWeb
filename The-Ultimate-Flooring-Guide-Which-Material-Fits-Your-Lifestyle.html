The Ultimate Flooring Guide

Which Material Fits Your Lifestyle?

Your floors are the hardest-working part of your home. They handle muddy paws, dropped coffee mugs, and thousands of footsteps every day. But with so many options — from the timeless beauty of oak to the modern toughness of vinyl — how do you choose?

At Cornerstone Constructs, we believe the best floor isn't just the one that looks good — it’s the one that stands up to your daily life. Here is our guide to picking the perfect surface.

3 Questions to Ask Before You Buy

  1. Who is walking on it? Big dogs, toddlers, or a "shoes-off" household?
  2. Where is it going? Moisture-heavy bathroom or cozy dry bedroom?
  3. What is the "Forever" factor? Want something you can refinish in 20 years, or a budget-friendly update?

Our Flooring Options: The Breakdown

1. Luxury Vinyl Plank (LVP) — The "Life-Proof" Choice

Looks exactly like wood but made from heavy-duty polymers.

  • Pros: 100% waterproof, scratch-resistant, soft underfoot.
  • Cons: Doesn’t have the same prestige or resale value as real hardwood.
  • Best For: Busy families with pets, basements, and high-traffic areas.

2. Hardwood (Solid vs Engineered) — The "Timeless Investment" Choice

Solid hardwood can be refinished multiple times. Engineered offers better stability in humid climates.

  • Pros: Unmatched beauty and highest resale value.
  • Cons: Can scratch and is sensitive to standing water.
  • Best For: Living rooms, dining rooms, and forever homes.

3. Laminate Flooring — The "High-Style, Low-Cost" Choice

Photographic layer that mimics wood or stone.

  • Pros: Very affordable and scratch-resistant.
  • Cons: Not fully waterproof — seams can swell if water sits.
  • Best For: Beautiful wood look on a tighter budget.

4. Tiles (Ceramic vs Porcelain) — The "Indestructible" Choice

Porcelain is denser and tougher than ceramic.

  • Pros: Best moisture resistance for wet zones.
  • Cons: Cold underfoot (radiant heat helps) and can be slippery.
  • Best For: Bathrooms, kitchens, and laundry rooms.

5. Carpet — The "Ultimate Comfort" Choice

Warm, soft, and quiet.

  • Pros: Excellent sound insulation and safest for kids/seniors.
  • Cons: Traps allergens and needs regular deep cleaning.
  • Best For: Bedrooms, home theaters, and nurseries.

🛠 We Also Do Repair Only

Cracked tile, deep hardwood scratch, or stained carpet? We can repair just the damaged area and blend it seamlessly — saving you thousands.

💡 Pro-Tips for Your New Floors

  • Curb Appeal Trick: Use the same flooring throughout the main floor for a seamless, spacious feel.
  • Acclimation is Key: Let hardwood and laminate sit in your home for a few days before installation.
  • Underlayment Secret: A quality underlayment makes even budget flooring feel premium and warmer.
