The Ultimate Guide to Picking Your Roofing Material

Everything You Need to Know — Asphalt, Metal, TPO & Euroshield

Choosing a new roof is a big deal, but it doesn't have to be complicated. If you feel like you’re looking at a bunch of rectangles and metal sheets and don't know the difference — this guide is for you.

We’ve broken down the four main materials we offer so you can find the perfect match for your North Bay home.

4 Factors to Consider Before You Choose

  1. How long do I plan to live here? Are you staying for 5 years or 50?
  2. What is my weather like? Do you deal with heavy hail, high winds, or intense summer heat?
  3. What is my budget? Are you looking for the lowest upfront cost or the best long-term value?
  4. How much do I care about "The Look"? Do you want a classic neighborhood look or something that stands out?

Our Roofing Materials: The Breakdown

1. Asphalt Shingles — The "Classic Reliable" Choice

Asphalt shingles are the most popular roofing material in North America. They are made of fiberglass mats coated in asphalt and ceramic granules.

  • Pros: Most affordable upfront cost, huge variety of colors, and very easy to repair if a single shingle gets damaged.
  • Cons: Shortest lifespan (usually 15–25 years) and can be prone to "blow-offs" during very high winds.
  • Best For: Budget-conscious homeowners or those planning to sell their home in the next few years.

2. Metal Roofing — The "One and Done" Choice

Modern metal roofs aren't just for barns anymore. They come in sleek panels that give homes a clean, high-end look.

  • Pros: Extremely durable (lasts 40–70 years), fire-resistant, and great for energy bills because they reflect the sun's heat away from your house.
  • Cons: Higher initial cost than asphalt and can be noisier during heavy rain (though proper insulation fixes this!).
  • Best For: Homeowners who want a "forever roof" and want to save money on monthly cooling/heating bills.

3. Flat / TPO Roofing — The "Modern Minimalist" Choice

TPO (Thermoplastic Polyolefin) is a single-ply white membrane used primarily on flat or very low-slope roofs.

  • Pros: Naturally waterproof (no seams for water to leak through) and highly reflective, which keeps flat-roofed homes much cooler in the summer.
  • Cons: Only works on flat or low-slope surfaces; not designed for traditional "pointy" roofs.
  • Best For: Modern homes, additions, or garages with flat rooflines where water drainage is the #1 priority.

4. Euroshield® — The "Indestructible" Choice

Euroshield is a premium "rubber" roofing made from 95% recycled tires. It’s designed to look exactly like expensive slate or wood shakes but with way more muscle.

  • Pros: Practically "hail-proof." It’s incredibly quiet, eco-friendly, and won’t crack, rot, or warp like real wood or stone.
  • Cons: Premium price point (similar to metal) and requires specialized installers.
  • Best For: Homeowners in storm-heavy areas who want a high-end "luxury" look without the high-end maintenance.

Comparison at a Glance

Material Lifespan Cost Best Feature
Asphalt Shingles 15–25 Years $ Budget-Friendly
Metal Roofing 40–70 Years $$$ Extreme Longevity
Flat / TPO 20–30 Years $$ Best for Flat Roofs
Euroshield® 50+ Years $$$ Hail & Impact Resistance
