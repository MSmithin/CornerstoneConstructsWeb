Foundation 101

A Guide to What’s Holding Up Your Home

When you’re looking at a house, you see the beautiful siding and the sturdy roof. But the most important part of your home is the part you can’t see: the foundation. A strong foundation keeps your home level, your doors from sticking, and your basement dry.

Whether you are building new, adding an extension, or worried about your current home’s "bones," here is everything you need to know about the four main types of foundations.

Why Foundation Health Matters

  • Prevent Costly Repairs: Small cracks today can lead to major structural issues tomorrow.
  • Property Value: A certified, sturdy foundation is the first thing a home inspector looks for.
  • Energy Efficiency: A well-sealed foundation keeps out dampness and drafts, lowering your bills.

Our Foundation Types: The Breakdown

1. Full Basement — The "Extra Space" Choice

Dug deep (usually 8+ feet) to create usable living or storage space.

  • Pros: Adds significant square footage and easy access to plumbing & electrical.
  • Cons: Most expensive and requires excellent waterproofing.
  • Best For: Homeowners who want extra living space (gym, workshop, etc.).

2. Crawl Space — The "Best of Both Worlds" Choice

Raises the home 2–3 feet with short foundation walls.

  • Pros: More affordable than a full basement, easy access to utilities.
  • Cons: Can become damp or attract pests if not properly encapsulated.
  • Best For: High-moisture areas or those wanting utility access without full basement cost.

3. Concrete Slab-on-Grade — The "Simple & Sturdy" Choice

Thick concrete poured directly on prepared ground.

  • Pros: Most cost-effective, quick to install, no basement flooding risk.
  • Cons: No extra storage space; plumbing is buried in concrete.
  • Best For: Budget builds or mild climates with shallow frost lines.

4. Pier & Beam — The "High & Dry" Choice

Concrete piers support wooden beams that hold up the house.

  • Pros: Excellent for sloped lots and flood-prone areas, great airflow.
  • Cons: Floors can feel bouncy if not engineered well.
  • Best For: Uneven terrain or areas with minor flooding risk.

🛠 We Don't Just Build — We Repair

Foundations need ongoing care as soil shifts and water moves. We specialize in:

  • Crack repair and sealing
  • Foundation leveling
  • Waterproofing & drainage solutions

💡 Pro-Tips for Homeowners

  • The Penny Test: Hairline cracks are often normal settling. If a crack is wide enough to fit a penny or is horizontal, call a professional.
  • Watch Your Gutters: Clean eavestroughs prevent water from pooling around the foundation — the #1 cause of settling.
  • Landscape Smart: Grade soil to slope away from the house (minimum 5°). This prevents thousands in water damage.
