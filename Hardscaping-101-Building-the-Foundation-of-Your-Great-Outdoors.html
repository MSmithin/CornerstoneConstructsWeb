Hardscaping 101

Building the Foundation of Your Great Outdoors

When we talk about "landscaping," most people think of plants and grass. But Hardscaping is the actual bones of your yard. It’s the stone, brick, and concrete that turn a patch of dirt into a functional living space.

At Cornerstone Constructs, we specialize in professional hardscaping that doesn't just look beautiful on day one — it’s built to stay level and sturdy through years of changing seasons.

Why Professional Hardscaping is a Game Changer

  • Instant Curb Appeal: A crisp paved driveway or stone walkway instantly elevates your home’s appearance.
  • Functional Space: Turns sloped or unusable areas into practical outdoor living zones.
  • Prevents Erosion: Manages water runoff and protects your foundation and lawn.

Our Hardscaping Services: The Breakdown

1. Walkways & Paved Paths — The "Welcome Home" Choice

Classic brick pavers, natural stone, or modern concrete options.

  • Eliminates muddy "cow paths" and keeps dirt out of your house
  • Expert base preparation ensures they stay level for years
  • Best For: Front entrances, garden paths, and backyard connections

2. Driveways — The "First Impression" Choice

High-strength paved or interlocking stone surfaces built for heavy loads.

  • Eliminates potholes and winter ice issues
  • Proper drainage slopes water away from your garage and foundation
  • Best For: Homes with multiple vehicles or tight turning areas

3. Retaining Walls — The "Landscape Rescue" Choice

Engineered walls that transform sloped yards into usable space.

  • Creates flat terraces for gardens, patios, or play areas
  • Proper drainage and reinforcement prevent leaning or collapse
  • Best For: Sloped properties and erosion control

💡 Pro-Tips for a High-End Look

  • Curb Appeal Secret: Match your walkway stone or brick to your home’s exterior accents (chimney, foundation) for a cohesive designer look.
  • Lighting Matters: Add integrated low-voltage LED path lighting for beauty and safety at night.
  • Maintenance is Minimal: Use polymeric sand in joints to stop weeds and keep pavers looking new for years.
