The Ultimate Guide to Eavestroughs

Protecting Your Home’s Foundation from the Ground Up

Most homeowners don’t think about their eavestroughs (gutters) until they see a waterfall pouring over their front door. But your eavestroughs are actually your home’s first line of defense.

Why Replace Your Old Gutters?

  • Protects Your Foundation: Moves thousands of gallons of water safely away from your basement.
  • Prevents Siding Stains: Stops splash-back from ruining your siding.
  • Eliminates Ice Dams: Proper drainage reduces dangerous ice buildup in winter.
  • Boosts Curb Appeal: Clean, straight gutters make your entire home look well-maintained and more valuable.

Our Eavestrough Materials: The Breakdown

1. Seamless Aluminum Eavestrough — The "Modern Standard"

Custom-made on-site to fit your home perfectly with no joints to leak.

  • Pros: Lightweight, rust-proof, available in any color to match your trim, nearly zero leak risk.
  • Cons: Can dent from heavy impact.
  • Best For: Most homeowners — perfect balance of price, performance, and style.

2. Steel (Galvalume / Galvanized) — The "Heavy Duty" Choice

Built tough for extreme conditions.

  • Pros: Extremely strong, handles heavy snow/ice loads, excellent temperature stability.
  • Cons: Heavier, can eventually rust if coating is damaged.
  • Best For: Brutal winters and heavy snow areas.

3. Specialty Copper Eavestrough — The "Luxury Heirloom" Choice

Develops a beautiful natural patina over time.

  • Pros: Lasts 70–100+ years, never rusts, dramatically increases home value.
  • Cons: Highest upfront cost.
  • Best For: Historic homes, luxury builds, or those wanting a premium statement.

Comparison at a Glance

Feature Seamless Aluminum Steel (Galvalume) Specialty Copper
Lifespan 20–30 Years 30–50 Years 70+ Years
Rust Resistance Excellent Very Good Immortal

💡 Pro-Tips for the Savvy Homeowner

  • Curb Appeal Secret: Match your eavestroughs to your fascia color for a sleek “disappearing” look, or match your window trim to make the architecture pop.
  • Downspouts Matter: Go with oversized 3x4 downspouts — they clog less and handle heavy rain better.
  • Leaf Guards: If you have trees nearby, leaf guards turn a messy twice-a-year chore into a “never again” task. They pay for themselves quickly.
